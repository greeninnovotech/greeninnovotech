Ludhiana — Mountains of Waste

Around 11,00,000 kg — that's the quantity of garbage the city of Ludhiana produces in a single day, the highest among 13 Municipal Corporations in Punjab. Nearly 3,074 tonnes of waste is collected in Punjab every day.

Our Innovation

Solar Powered Plastic Pyrolysis Plant

Our Mission

To upcycle the entire plastic waste generated using Renewable Energy in a non-polluting, Eco-Sustainable way — assimilating plastic waste in an ecologically responsible manner every single day.

🧪

The Problem with Plastics

Plastics have outgrown most man-made materials and are under environmental scrutiny. By 2015, approximately 6,300 Mt of plastic waste had been generated. Around 9% was recycled, 12% incinerated, and 79% accumulated in landfills or the natural environment.

🔬

What is Pyrolysis?

A system where non-biodegradable or slow-degradable plastic is assimilated through pyrolysis — converting it into a form which is controllable, storable and reusable. The challenge was to develop a process that consumes no fossil fuel or electricity while being more efficient in terms of input-to-output energy ratio.

☀️

Solar Plastic Pyrolysis — Our Approach

Using Solar energy to initiate and propagate the reaction in the reactor for processing plastic garbage, capturing & utilising exo-energy (normally wasted) via a Concave Reflector and heat capturing mechanism. This exo-energy can further be used to generate electricity sustainably.

India receives 4–7 kWh/m² of Solar Irradiance per day (MNRE)
Present PV solar panels produce 150–200 W/h at max 11% efficiency
India has 3,615 hours of sunlight per year — ~9.9 hrs/day (Punjab)
Potential to capture 39.6–69.3 kW/day/m² across the Indian sub-continent
Multiple stacked systems all using the sun's irradiance simultaneously
Zero fossil fuel. Zero electricity input. Self-sustaining energy loop.

How It Works

The Waste Plastic Recycling Process

A systematic 7-step pathway from waste collection to clean energy generation and new products.

1
🚛

Collection

Plastic waste collected from curbside programs, drop-off centres, and waste collection companies from various urban sources.

2
🔄

Sorting

Sorted by type and colour — PET, HDPE, LDPE etc. Different plastics have different properties and must be processed separately.

3
⚙️

Shredding

Sorted plastic is shredded into small pieces — dramatically increasing surface area for efficient processing and recycling.

4
💧

Washing

Shredded plastic is washed thoroughly to remove dirt and debris, ensuring the output material is clean and safe for use.

5
🔥

Solar Pyrolysis

Solar concentrators heat the plastic in an oxygen-free reactor. Thermal decomposition converts it into gas, oil and char.

6
🛢️

Product Extraction

Paraffin (high calorific value fuel), synthetic gas and liquid fuel are extracted — valuable, storable and reusable outputs.

7
⚡

Energy Generation

Exo-energy from the reactor is captured and converted to electricity. Sustainable, reusable, with very long shelf life.

✓
🌿

Zero Landfill

End result: freed landmass, no landfill burden, saved environment and a circular economy that pays for itself.

✅ Benefits

Reduces plastic waste going to landfills — cutting methane emissions
Conserves natural resources — no new petroleum extraction required
Reduces ocean and river pollution — protects marine ecosystems
Generates clean electricity as a by-product of the process
Creates employment in the recycling and clean-energy sector
Frees up urban land currently buried under legacy waste mountains

⚠️ Challenges Addressed

Difficulty collecting all plastic waste — solved by modular local plants
Sorting complexity — addressed through pre-processing sorting line
Not all plastics recyclable conventionally — pyrolysis handles mixed waste
High energy cost of conventional pyrolysis — eliminated by solar input
Public awareness — addressed through partnerships and demonstration plants
Fossil fuel dependency — completely removed by solar-thermal design
